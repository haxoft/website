    Weekly Log

    13th - 19th February '17

    Another week - another 7 days without a customer meeting. Originally, we we expected to see Prof. Gaedke on Wednesday. Unfortunately, he did not have time for a meeting. It is quite hard to make significant progress in a project without showing it to somebody who is not directly involved in it. We have been missing a more frequent input from an "external viewer" along the development. As we also experienced, it is motivating to know that the customer is interested in our product and being able to discuss some details with him is crucial to keep the right direction during development. Having that in mind, weekly meetings would be a nice thing to have. This way, we would be able to have more frequent feedback on how we performed/are performing and the adjustments we should make. We are however still doing our best to deal with the current conditions and situation. After all, adjusting to unexpected situations is a key skill of any development team. It is worth mentioning that we very much appreciate the possibility to be consulting issues regarding our emerging product with our prof., as his planspiel role is twofold: our counselor and the first customer at the same time.
    After the original Wednesday meeting, which did not take place, we received information from the prof. that the next customer meeting should be on Tuesday next week. We are looking forward to it!

    Regardless of whether the meeting took place or not, we continued with the development of the HXT and the corresponding tasks. Having in mind the fact that the planspiel is coming to its end, we started to sketch out our business plan. Our business model served as a good foundation for this purpose. Within this week, we made some notes about different thoughts we collected and drafted the concrete sections. In order to have a first draft of our business plan, we will have to do some more research and continue with the task during the next week.

    As we already mentioned, the development of the project still continues. We managed to add user management to the backend and the frontend of the application, as well as the corresponding session management. This functionality allows the HXT to recognize different users (with different access rights) and to serve resources to these users accordingly. One part of this functionality that was especially time consuming was the authentication of users (user agents) making requests to the HXT plugin. The user management is handled at the "front-line" by Atlassian and our plugin just simply receives a "claim" containing the information about the user making the request. This kind of delegated authentication was a bit confusing at the beginning, since it comprises 3 entities (Atlassian, HXT, User agent) sending and receiving a JWT.
    To close this entry with some good news, we are happy to announce that we are now very close to the first automated extraction of some important proposal requirements! We have found the first two regular expressions (which are both very big and ugly creatures) and we are currently trying to insert them in the extraction module of the HXT plugin.

As a starting point our plan determines some easy question about our interview partner. So how long she or he worked already as a project planner and what her or his current position is named. With this introduction knowledge we can on one side help the interviewed one to get started in the answering flow with easy to answer questions and on the other side we can later on better compare the different results from the interviews - so somehow classify them a bit. After knowing their processes or may rather phases of creating a plan for a new project, we want to ask them detailed questions on each phase. Moreover we want to ask them for the tools they use for project planning in each of those phases and if there are pain points anywhere. Those pain points of course do not need to be only in a software, but also in other structural pursuits. And maybe some phases are poorly supported?

In the end of the interview we want to them to grade each phase or tool with 1 to 5. This will hopefully also help us to uncover pain points they even do not directly mention.

After many discussions and brainstorming sessions, we have finally found common ground and decided for one name. As you probably know now, we have decided to name our team and company - 'Haxoft'. But why Haxoft?
    Haxoft is a composition of two words - hack and soft. While the reason for choosing soft is probably obvious, the reason why we use 'hack' might not be so. The word „hack“ is used in many contexts nowadays, but originally it means „to produce an ingenious piece of work that solves a meaningful problem“. And that’s what we stand for: game-changing and reliable software that will give you an advantage over your competitors. Curiously enough, the name "Haxoft" was one of the first names that came to our mind. One might, therefore, say we are using a rather spontaneous company name. To give you an example of some of the names that we were considering, we provide you below a brief list with some of them:

    • Hexoft
    • Coconut Technologies
    • BinaryCase
    • BinaryFactory
    • nerdsco
    • Physalis Systems

    As you can see, we had a few ideas for the company name and each of them had its own story or "reason" behind it. As mentioned before, the better "ground story" and the most consensual turned out to be Haxoft.
